About

A Voice Born of Late Nights and Low Light

Sydney Small sings the way a candle burns — slow, warm, and impossible to look away from. Every note carries the weight of a life spent chasing melody.

The Journey

From the Choir Loft to the Lounge

Chapter One

Roots in Gospel

Sydney's first stage was a private event, singing alongside a grandmother who taught her that a voice is a gift meant to be given away. Those early Sunday harmonies still live in every phrase she sings. From these humble beginnings, she discovered her passion for musical theatre, performing in chapter one productions and private events throughout her youth. These formative years shaped her distinctive artistic voice and instilled a deep commitment to sharing her gift with audiences of all sizes.

Chapter Two

Finding Jazz

A borrowed Ella Fitzgerald record changed everything. Sydney spent years studying phrasing in smoky clubs, sitting in with local trios and learning to bend a lyric until it broke your heart.

Chapter Three

A Sound of Her Own

Today, Sydney blends soul, blues, and classic jazz into something entirely hers — a sound that fills a room without ever raising its voice.

Artistic Vision

Music as an Intimate Conversation

Sydney believes a great performance isn't about filling a room with volume — it's about drawing a room in close. Her sets are built like conversations: patient, honest, full of pauses that let a lyric land.

Whether she's fronting a full band or singing solo over a single piano, the goal is the same — to make every listener feel like the song was written just for them, in that room, on that night.

Sydney studied at The New School in New York City, where she focused on vocal performance, music, and the business of the entertainment industry. During her time there, she immersed herself in the city's vibrant live music scene, honing her craft while performing with jazz trios, collaborating with accomplished musicians, and developing her signature blend of soul, blues, jazz, and pop.
